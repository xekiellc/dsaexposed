What Is the Red-Green Axis?

The Red-Green Axis describes the documented political coalition between the radical left — "Red," for Marxist and socialist organizations — and political Islam — "Green," for Islamist organizations. The two ideological traditions have little in common theologically or culturally. What unites them is a shared opposition: to Western civilization, to American power, to capitalism, and to Israel.

This is not a conspiracy theory. It is a documented pattern of shared endorsements, co-organized events, coordinated funding flows, and organizational partnerships that has accelerated in the United States since 2016 and reached its apex in the 2026 NYC primary sweep, where DSA-endorsed candidates were simultaneously funded by anti-AIPAC PACs with Muslim donor networks and endorsed by CAIR-aligned organizations.

The bridge between the two movements is the BDS movement — officially endorsed by National DSA in 2017, co-promoted by CAIR, organized on campuses by AMP, and supported by all seven DSA members of Congress. BDS is where the Red and the Green formally meet.
